# Northwind Capital — Market Entry Options

## Executive summary

Northwind is evaluating entry into the mid-market advisory segment. We assessed three options against capital intensity, time to revenue, and execution risk. We recommend a partnership-led entry, assuming the partner's pipeline holds and integration costs stay within the modeled range.

## Situation

Northwind has strong brand recognition in the enterprise segment but limited presence mid-market. The segment is growing at approximately 9% annually, though margins are thinner and the competitive set is more fragmented.

## Options

We considered: (1) organic build-out, (2) acquisition of a regional firm, and (3) a partnership-led entry with a revenue-share structure. Organic build-out carries the lowest capital outlay but the longest time to revenue. Acquisition is fastest but the most capital-intensive and integration-heavy.

## Recommendation

We recommend the partnership-led entry. This balances time to revenue against capital exposure, assuming the partner's pipeline materializes as represented and that the revenue-share terms remain as discussed. We project a positive contribution within approximately four quarters, subject to those assumptions.

## Risk and assumptions

Key assumptions: partner pipeline holds, integration costs stay within the modeled range, and no material change in segment growth. The principal risks are partner concentration and slower-than-expected ramp. These are estimates and are subject to revision as diligence proceeds; they are not guarantees of outcome.
